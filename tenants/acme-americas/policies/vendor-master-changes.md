# Vendor master change control policy

## 1. Scope

This policy governs changes to the vendor master record: adding a vendor, changing
banking or remittance details, and deactivating a vendor.

## 2. Segregation of duties

The person who requests a vendor master change may not be the person who approves it.
Banking-detail changes require a second approver independent of the requester and of
the invoice-approval chain for that vendor.

## 3. Bank-detail changes

A change to a vendor's banking details is verified against a contact on file through
a channel independent of the change request (a known phone number, not a number
supplied in the request). No payment is released to changed banking details until the
verification is recorded on the case.

## 4. Deactivation

A vendor with no activity for four consecutive quarters is flagged for deactivation
review. Deactivation blocks new invoices but preserves history for audit.

## 5. Records

Every vendor master change records the requester, the approver(s), the verification
performed, and the before/after values, retained for the statutory audit period.
