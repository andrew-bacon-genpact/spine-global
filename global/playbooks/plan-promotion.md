# Plan promotion playbook

How a learned plan moves from drafted to routing live cases, and how a case type
climbs the autonomy ladder.

## 1. Autonomy rungs

A case type sits on one of three rungs:

- **watch** — the plan runs in shadow; its proposed disposition is recorded but not
  applied. Used to gather precision evidence.
- **propose** — the plan runs and presents its disposition for a single approval
  click before applying.
- **auto** — the plan applies its disposition without human touch, within its gates.

## 2. Promotion criteria

A case type is promoted a rung only on evidence: a minimum case volume at or above a
precision floor, measured on the current rung, with no unresolved incident. Promotion
is a reviewed change like any other.

## 3. Demotion

A case type is demoted immediately on a confirmed incident or a precision drop below
its floor, and stays demoted until the cause is understood and the plan corrected.

## 4. Records

Every promotion and demotion records the evidence, the approver, and the effective
date, so the autonomy of every case type is auditable back to the decision that
granted it.
